# Phase 4 plan — the crawl

Implementation plan for Phase 4 of [the osrlib spec](spec.md). Phase 4 delivers the framework layer: the adventure container with its base town, the multi-level dungeon grid with keyed areas, the crawl party with marching order, the exploration turn loop (movement, time, light, rest, provisions, searching, doors, traps, wandering monsters), the encounter procedure (surprise, distance, reaction, parley, evasion and pursuit), the range-track battle state machine with the default monster action policy, the `GameSession` command/event API with player and referee views, and save/load with the migration framework. It closes the standing forward references: geometry-to-target mapping for areas lands on the combat space, location-bound effects gain real cells (the burning-oil pool, *silence*'s stationary form, *web*'s blocking cube), the battle machine detects spell disruption, invokes morale triggers, and consumes the Phase 3 marker conditions and battle-bound spell effects (*haste*, *invisibility*, *mirror image*, *confusion*, the *protection from evil* melee ban), the exploration loop consumes the Phase 3 light/infravision effect data and gates memorization behind rest, the session owns the entity-ID allocator, the effects registry, the death records, and the clock context the kernel has been promising since Phase 2, and `load(save)` versus `replay(seed, commands)` becomes the standing test the Phase 2 goldens deferred. Milestone from the spec: a scripted delve — enter from town, explore two dungeon levels, spring a trap, fight, flee with dropped treasure (hand-placed via keyed-area content; generated hoards arrive in Phase 5), rest, return to safety — replayable from seed + commands and restorable from a save.

## Scope

In scope:

- `tools/srd_compile/encounter_tables.py` — the dungeon encounter tables by level from `Dungeon_Encounters.md`, with `overrides/encounter_tables.json` for the "Basic Adventures" typo and kin; `tools/srd_compile/combat_tables.py` grows the reaction table (source `Encounters.md`); `tools/srd_compile/equipment.py` grows structured exploration params for the gear the crawl consumes (torch, lantern, oil, tinder box, rations, iron spikes)
- `src/osrlib/data/` — generated `encounter_tables.json` with typed loader `load_encounter_tables()`; `combat_tables.json` and `equipment.json` regenerate
- `core/tables.py` — reaction-table lookup; `core/combat.py` — `roll_reaction`; `core/classes.py` — the thief d%/in-6 skill check and the demi-human X-in-6 detection check the exploration procedures consume
- `core/ruleset.py` — the three remaining Phase 4 flags: `deprivation_penalties`, `aoe_friendly_fire`, `formation_width_limit` — the register's first true documented adaptations
- `crawl/` — the new package, per the spec architecture: `events.py`, `commands.py` (new module, `validation.py` precedent), `party.py`, `adventure.py`, `dungeon.py`, `exploration.py`, `encounter.py`, `battle.py`, `session.py`
- `osrlib/persistence.py` — save/load, the migration framework, replay, and the load-equals-replay guarantee
- `osrlib/messages.py` and `core/events.py` — the crawl event set, its templates, and the kernel reaction event
- Tests: data pipeline and table fidelity, dungeon/exploration/encounter/battle/session/persistence suites, property and statistical tests, and the milestone delve golden

Out of scope (deferred to the phase that consumes them):

- Treasure generation, magic items, and hoards (5) — the milestone's dropped and recovered treasure is hand-placed keyed-area content; the session's recovered-treasure and defeated-monster ledgers accumulate now as the Phase 5 award inputs
- NPC adventuring parties (5) — the encounter tables compile their Basic/Expert Adventurer rows faithfully as structured `npc_party` entries, and the wandering procedure re-rolls them until Phase 5 builds the parties (they need treasure types and magic items)
- End-of-adventure XP awards and the `xp_award_timing` flag (5) — the base town anchors return-to-safety now and the ledgers record the inputs; the award procedure and flag ride with treasure (1 gp = 1 XP needs treasure values). The `AwardXP` referee command ships now — it is the spec's referee-surface example and Phase 5's award will drive it
- The `magic_item_death_save` flag (5) — needs magic items; the destructive-death path already exists
- Selling treasure and paid healing in town (5) — both need the treasure economy; Phase 4's town offers safe rest and equipment purchase through the Phase 1 kernel
- The dungeon stocking and unguarded-treasure tables as data (5) — nothing in Phase 4 consumes them (the milestone dungeon is authored, not generated); they compile alongside treasure generation, which their treasure columns need
- Wilderness and waterborne adventuring, including the wilderness/waterborne encounter, evasion, and pursuit tables (out of 1.0) — the dungeon procedure only; High-Level party types are wilderness-table entries and defer with them
- Retainers, hirelings, mercenaries, specialists (out of 1.0) — morale stays monster-only; loyalty checks have no subject
- Climbing, swimming, drowning, getting lost, and other vertical/wilderness hazards — `thief_skill_check` ships the climb roll for à la carte use, but the grid has no vertical model; the procedures stay game territory
- Subduing, bombing, unstable surfaces, underwater combat (out of 1.0, Phase 2 precedent); darkness combat penalties (no RAW numbers — the caller-supplied situational modifier is the surface)
- Monster and NPC spellcasting in the action policy (5) — monster casting tags are `manual` data; the default policy never casts, and games or LLM referees substitute policies per encounter side; NPC parties cast through the character model in Phase 5
- Procedural dungeon generation (out of 1.0) — the adventure model is authored content

## Work items

### 1. Encounter and reaction tables — `tools/srd_compile/encounter_tables.py`, `core/tables.py`

- `compile_encounter_tables(srd_dir)` slots into `__main__.py` with the established conventions. Source: `Dungeon_Encounters.md` — two pipe tables carrying six level columns (`Level 1`, `2`, `3`, `4–5`, `6-7`, `8+` — note the en-dash/hyphen inconsistency in the printed headers) of twenty d20 rows each. Output `encounter_tables.json`: per column, twenty `EncounterTableRow` entries — `roll`, `entry`, `count_dice` (the parenthesized dice, `1` compiling as fixed 1). Every row's number-appearing dice are the table's own — pinned per the SRD's note and the spec: the table value overrides the monster description's.
- `entry` is one of three structured forms, pinned:
    - `monster`: a template id (or id tuple, below) resolving against `monsters.json` — the compiler asserts every reference resolves, exactly like *conjure elemental*'s in Phase 3. The cell-name → id mapping is hand-curated in a `_TABLE_ENTRIES` map (the `_SPELL_MECHANICS` precedent), because the printed links are unresolvable: they point at per-variant pages that don't exist (`Beetle, Oil` → the `Beetle, Giant` page's `oil_beetle`), use raw commas and single encoding against double-encoded filenames (`Doppelg%C3%A4nger`), and disagree with variant naming (`Cat, Mountain Lion` → `mountain_lion`, `Wolf` → `normal_wolf`, `Dwarf` → `dwarf_monster`, `Neanderthal` → `neanderthal_caveman`). Survey confirmed every one of the 120 cells resolves to a compiled id.
    - `monster` with a variant pool: cells naming a packed-variant page without picking one (`Veteran (2d4)` over `veteran_1..3`, `Vampire (1d4)` over `vampire_7..9`, `Hellhound (2d4)` over `hellhound_3..7`) compile with the full id tuple; at spawn time each individual picks uniformly from the pool on the wandering stream — pinned (RAW leaves the pick to the referee; per-individual uniform is deterministic and matches the pages' "3 to 7" spreads).
    - `npc_party`: kind `basic` or `expert` with the printed count dice (`1d4+4`, `1d6+3`). The "Basic Adventures" typo — row 2 of the **Level 3 column** in the first table — normalizes via `overrides/encounter_tables.json` with provenance; the spec names this exact typo as the overrides mechanism's example. The Level 4–5/6-7/8+ tables' singular "Expert Adventurer" label normalizes in the same override family.
    - The two hydra cells (`Hydra 1d4+4HD (1)`, `Hydra 1d4+8HD (1)`) compile as a variant pool with `variant_dice` (`1d4+4` → `hydra_5..8`, `1d4+8` → `hydra_9..12`): the printed HD dice roll on the wandering stream selects the template — pinned.
- `compile_combat_tables` grows the reaction table from `Encounters.md` (added to `SOURCE_PAGES`): the five 2d6 bands (2-, 3–5, 6–8, 9–11, 12+). `CombatTables` grows `reaction: ReactionTable`; `reaction_result(total) -> ReactionResult` in `core/tables.py` clamps totals below 2 and above 12 into the outer bands (the table's own "2 or less" / "12 or more"). `roll_reaction(*, modifier=0, stream) -> ReactionRollResult` in `core/combat.py` beside `check_morale`: 2d6 + modifier, emitting `ReactionRolledEvent` (`encounter.reaction.rolled`, **referee** visibility — players learn reactions from behavior, the morale precedent). The CHA modifier is the caller's to supply from `npc_reaction_modifier`; the crawl's encounter procedure passes the `"encounter"` stream.
- The `EncounterTable`/`EncounterTableRow` models live in `core/tables.py` beside the reaction models — pinned for layering: `osrlib/data/` loaders import their model homes and `core` modules import the loaders, so a `crawl/` model home would give `load_encounter_tables()` a core → data → crawl transitive import. The crawl layer consumes the models; it doesn't define them.
- Level-to-column mapping, pinned: a dungeon level's table is its level number clamped into the printed bands — 1, 2, 3, 4–5, 6–7, and everything 8 or deeper on `8+`. A `LevelSpec` may override with an inline custom table (same row model) for content that wants its own list; the compiled tables are the default.

### 2. Exploration gear params — `tools/srd_compile/equipment.py`

- `GearTemplate` grows `params: dict[str, int | str | bool] = {}` (additive), populated by a hand-curated `_GEAR_MECHANICS` map, the `_CLASS_ABILITIES` precedent. Pinned from `Adventuring_Gear.md`: torch `{burn_turns: 6, light_radius_feet: 30, brightness: "flame"}` ("casts light in a 30' radius and burns for 1 hour (6 turns)"); lantern `{burn_turns_per_flask: 24, light_radius_feet: 30, brightness: "flame"}`; oil flask `{fuels_lantern: true}` (its splash facet and pool params already ship); tinder box `{light_chance_in_six: 2}` (one round per attempt); rations `{ration: true}` on both kinds — the 7-day lot quantity already ships via `_GEAR_LOT_SIZES`, so no duplicate day count, and the standard/iron distinction rides the ids; iron spikes `{wedges_doors: true}`; waterskin `{water_container: true}`; thieves' tools `{required_for: "open_locks"}`.
- Lighting a torch or lantern consumes the consumable (one torch from the lot, one oil flask) and attaches a `light`-kind effect to the bearer with the radius param and the turn-denominated duration, expiring with the reserved `exploration.torch.expired` code family. Extinguishing releases the effect and forfeits the remainder — pinned for both sources: no partial-burn bookkeeping (a game that wants candle-end accounting owns it); relighting starts a fresh consumable. The tinder box gates lighting when the party has no open flame: 2-in-6 per round on the exploration stream, RAW.

### 3. Kernel skill checks — `core/classes.py`

- `thief_skill_check(character, definition, skill, *, modifier_pct=0, stream) -> SkillCheckResult`: d% roll-under for the six percentile skills, 1d6-under-threshold for `hear_noise`; the row comes from `definition.thief_skills` at the character's level. Pick-pockets' over-5th-level penalty and the twice-the-target noticed rule ship in the result fields; its use is a game procedure. À la carte plain result, no events — the crawl procedures emit.
- `detection_check(chance_in_six, *, stream) -> DetectionResult` — the shared X-in-6 roll for searching, listening, and demi-human detection tags; `open_doors_check` already exists.
- Chance resolution, pinned: listening uses the thief's `hear_noise` row when present, else the class's `listening_at_doors` param, else 1-in-6; secret doors use `detect_secret_doors` (elf 2) else 1; room traps use `detect_room_traps` (dwarf 2) else 1; construction tricks use `detect_construction_tricks` (dwarf 2) else **zero** — the SRD grants the perception to dwarves alone, and "as a dwarf you can sense" has no baseline for others, unlike the universal 1-in-6 search chances which the SRD states for all PCs.

### 4. Ruleset — `core/ruleset.py`

Three new flags, defaults per the spec — the first entries in the register's documented-adaptations section (the SRD leaves all three to the referee):

- `deprivation_penalties` (default off) — consumption is tracked regardless (work item 8); with the flag on, the SRD's "at the referee's discretion, for example" list gets pinned defaults drawn from its own examples (attack penalties, reduced movement, more frequent rest, slow hit-point loss): after one full day without food or water, −1 to attack rolls (an engine-written modifier effect) and the rest cadence doubles (fatigue after three unrested turns instead of six); after two days, movement also halves; from the third day on, a daily 1d4 hit-point loss ticks on the effects stream. Water and food deprivation don't stack — the worse track applies. The register entry names the schedule as invented numbers over the SRD's open list.
- `aoe_friendly_fire` (default on) — areas overlapping a melee catch friends (work item 10's footprint rule); off means areas never include party members among a monster group's candidates.
- `formation_width_limit` (default on) — corridor width caps combatants fighting abreast (work item 10's rank model); off means every combatant may melee.

### 5. Commands, events, messages — `crawl/commands.py`, `crawl/events.py`, `osrlib/messages.py`

- Commands mirror the event conventions exactly: frozen pydantic models, a single-valued `command_type` Literal discriminator, dotted snake_case rejection codes, an `AnyCommand` discriminated union, `parse_command` returning `None` on unknown types. `CommandResult` (frozen): `accepted`, `rejections: tuple[Rejection, ...]`, `events: tuple[Event, ...]` — the Phase 1 `Rejection` model verbatim, as `validation.py` promised.
- The command census, pinned (names are the contract; per-command time, draws, and rejection codes are specified in their work items):
    - Exploration: `MoveParty(direction)`, `TurnParty(facing)`, `ReorderParty(order)` (marching order is state, so its only mutation path is a command), `OpenDoor(direction)`, `CloseDoor(direction)`, `ForceDoor(direction, character_id)`, `WedgeDoor(direction)`, `ListenAtDoor(direction, character_id)`, `PickLock(direction, character_id)`, `Search(character_id, kind ∈ {secret_doors, room_traps, construction})`, `InspectTreasure(character_id, feature_id)`, `RemoveTreasureTrap(character_id, feature_id)`, `TakeTreasure(feature_id)`, `DropItems(...)`, `LightSource(character_id, item)`, `ExtinguishSource(character_id)`, `EquipItem`/`UnequipItem`, `Rest(kind ∈ {turn, night, day})`, `PrepareSpells(character_id, selections)`, `CastSpell(character_id, spell_id, mode, reversed, targets)`, `TurnUndead(character_id)` (exploration/encounter modes; in battle it is a declaration kind), `UseStairs`, `EnterDungeon(dungeon_id)`, `TravelToTown`, `PurchaseEquipment(character_id, item_ids)`.
    - Encounter: `Parley(character_id)`, `Evade(drop ∈ {none, treasure, food})`, `EngageBattle`, `Wait`, plus `TurnUndead` — the one aggressive act with a pre-battle procedure of its own; every other offensive action (an attack, a hostile cast) is opened through `EngageBattle` and the first round's declarations, pinned.
    - Battle: `ResolveBattleRound(declarations)` — one command per round (work item 10).
    - Referee: `GrantItem`, `GrantCoins`, `AwardXP`, `SetFlag(key, value)`, `SpawnMonsters(template_id, count_dice, distance_feet)`, `SetDoorState`, `PlaceParty(location)`, `AdvanceTime(n, unit)`. Referee commands are logged and replayed like any other, per the spec.
- Session modes gate legality: `town`, `exploring`, `encounter`, `battle`, `game_over`; a wrong-mode command rejects with `session.command.wrong_mode`. The time-cost census, pinned in full (costs land through the odometer/clock per work item 8): zero-time — `TurnParty`, `ReorderParty`, `OpenDoor`, `CloseDoor`, `WedgeDoor`, `ForceDoor` (a moment of violence; the noise is the cost), `ListenAtDoor` (the once-per-door cap is the limiter), equip/unequip, `DropItems`, `ExtinguishSource`, `PurchaseEquipment` (town time is abstract), and all referee commands; one round — `CastSpell` and `TurnUndead` outside battle, `LightSource` (per tinder attempt too, RAW); one turn — `Search`, `InspectTreasure` (the SRD's searching stipulations name treasure traps), `RemoveTreasureTrap` (pinned symmetric; RAW is silent), `PickLock` (pinned; RAW is silent), `TakeTreasure` (RAW: "Filling a backpack or sack with treasure or other items takes 1 turn"), `Rest(turn)`; on the odometer — `MoveParty` per cell and `UseStairs` (one unexplored-cell cost, pinned); stated durations — `Rest(night)` 48 turns, `Rest(day)` 144, `PrepareSpells` 6, travel per content.
- Crawl events subclass the core `Event` base (same frozen/extra-ignore enforcement). `CRAWL_EVENT_CLASSES` joins the kernel tuple in an `ALL_EVENT_CLASSES` registry and an `AnyEvent` union in `crawl/events.py`; `parse_any_event` covers both and the session log uses it. The census (event — codes — visibility):
    - `PartyMovedEvent` (`exploration.party.moved` / `.blocked` / `.turned`, player), `LocationEnteredEvent` (`exploration.location.entered`, player — `location_kind ∈ {area, level, dungeon, town}` plus ids; the spec's listener example event), `DoorEvent` (`exploration.door.opened` / `.closed` / `.forced` / `.stuck` / `.wedged` / `.swung_shut` / `.unlocked`, player), `ListenedEvent` (`exploration.listen.heard` / `.silent`, player — the referee-side roll rides `DetectionRolledEvent`), `DetectionRolledEvent` (`exploration.detection.rolled`, **referee** — every search/listen die, rolled whether or not anything is there), `SearchCompletedEvent` (`exploration.search.found` / `.nothing`, player), `TrapEvent` (`exploration.trap.sprung` / `.safe` / `.found` / `.removed`, player; the 2-in-6 roll is referee), `ItemAcquiredEvent` (`exploration.item.acquired`, player — the spec's listener example), `ItemsDroppedEvent` (`exploration.item.dropped`, player), `LightEvent` (`exploration.light.lit` / `.extinguished` / `.failed` / `.expired`, player — the ledger's `EffectExpiredEvent` is referee-visibility, so when an advance returns a light-kind expiry the session appends the player-facing `.expired` with the source kind, pinned mechanism), `RestedEvent` (`exploration.rest.rested` / `.interrupted`, player), `FatigueEvent` (`exploration.fatigue.gained` / `.recovered`, player), `ProvisionsEvent` (`exploration.provisions.consumed` / `.short`, player), `WanderingCheckEvent` (`exploration.wandering.checked`, **referee**), `SpellsPreparedGateEvent` — no: preparation reuses the Phase 3 `SpellsMemorizedEvent`; the gate produces rejections, not events.

### 6. Adventure and dungeon models — `crawl/adventure.py`, `crawl/dungeon.py`

- Authored content is frozen; play mutates a state overlay — the template/instance split applied to space. `Adventure` (frozen): `name`, `description`, `hooks`, `town: TownSpec` (`name`, `description`, prose services list, `travel_turns: dict[dungeon_id, int]` — town-to-entrance travel cost, content-authored), `dungeons: tuple[DungeonSpec, ...]`. Content prose lives in these models — they are game content, not events; events carry ids and front ends resolve prose against the adventure.
- `DungeonSpec` (frozen): `id`, `name`, `levels: tuple[LevelSpec, ...]`. `LevelSpec` (frozen): `number` (1-based, rules-visible — it keys the encounter-table band), `width`, `height`, `edges`, `areas: tuple[AreaSpec, ...]`, `features`, `transitions`, `wandering: WanderingSpec` (`chance_in_six=1`, `interval_turns=2`, optional inline table override), `entrance: Position | None` (where `EnterDungeon` and town travel land; required on some level per adventure validation).
- Geometry, pinned as API convention: cells are 10' squares addressed `(x, y)` with `x` increasing east and `y` increasing south from the level's northwest corner; `Direction` is a `StrEnum` (`north`/`east`/`south`/`west`) with vector helpers. Edges are the single spatial truth for walls and doors: an `edges` map keyed by the canonical edge key (a cell plus `north` or `west`, so each physical edge has exactly one entry); `EdgeKind ∈ {open, wall, door}`. The level boundary is implicitly wall. `DoorSpec` (frozen, on a door edge): `kind ∈ {normal, secret}`, `stuck`, `locked`, `starts_open=False`.
- `AreaSpec` (frozen): `id`, `name`, `description`, `cells: tuple[Position, ...]`, plus content bindings: `encounter: KeyedEncounter | None` (monster template ids with counts or dice, optional fixed `alignment`, `aware: bool = False`, optional pinned stance), `features`, `trap: TrapSpec | None` (a room trap over the area), `flags_required` and kin stay game territory via listeners. Areas annotate the grid; cells not in any area are corridor.
- `FeatureSpec` (frozen, bound to a cell or area): `id`, `kind ∈ {treasure_cache, construction_trick, custom}` (stairs are `TransitionSpec`'s alone — no second home), for caches the hand-placed contents (item ids, coins) and an optional `trap: TrapSpec` (treasure trap) — the milestone's droppable, recoverable treasure. `TransitionSpec` (frozen, on a cell): `kind ∈ {stairs_up, stairs_down, trapdoor, chute}`, destination `(dungeon_id, level_number, position, facing)`; chutes are one-way.
- `TrapSpec` (frozen): `kind ∈ {room, treasure}`, `trigger ∈ {enter, open}`, `effect: TrapEffect` — `damage_dice`, `volley_dice` (the darts trap fires 1d6 projectiles, each rolling `damage_dice` — a count-times-damage form the dice grammar alone can't say), `save: SaveSpec | None` (category, negates or half), `condition` + duration, `transition` (pits and slides drop the victim), `manual` prose for the rest; `affects ∈ {triggerer, party}` (default triggerer). The example trap census from `Designing_a_Dungeon.md` (falling block 1d10 save-versus-petrification-negates, poison gas save-or-die, pit, scything blade 1d8 no save, darts 1d6 × 1d4, blindness 1d8 turns save-versus-spells) must all be expressible and are the fixture set.
- `DungeonState` (mutable, serialized): per-level explored cell set, door state (`open`, `wedged`, `discovered` for secret doors, `unlocked`), sprung/removed traps, found traps and construction tricks, emptied caches, dropped-item piles by cell, resolved keyed encounters, per-lock and per-target attempt memory (work item 8), and the party's `location` (`town`, or `(dungeon_id, level_number, x, y, facing)`).
- `validate_adventure(adventure, monsters, equipment) -> None` raises `ContentValidationError` on dangling references (transition targets, template ids, item ids, area cells out of bounds) — the fail-fast content gate the spec's error taxonomy names.

### 7. The crawl party — `crawl/party.py`

- `Party` (mutable, serialized): `members: list[Character]` where list order **is** marching order — pinned: no separate order field to desync; `reorder` swaps in place. Derived: `movement_rate(ruleset)` = the slowest living member's rate (SRD group rule); `ranks(width)` chunks living members in marching order (work item 10); the speaking character for reaction is whoever `Parley` names.
- `GameSession.new` assigns the ids `character.py` reserved: `character-NNNN` from the session's `IdAllocator` in party order — pinned prefix, closing the Phase 1 seam. Dead members stay in the party (their gear is carried state; excluding them is a game decision via referee commands) but never count toward movement rate, ranks, checks, or provisions.

### 8. The exploration turn loop — `crawl/exploration.py`

- **Time and movement.** The loop owns the odometer that reconciles feet-per-turn movement with the integer round clock, pinned: the session accrues movement in thirds-of-feet (integers — an unexplored cell costs 30 units, a previously explored cell 10, implementing the SRD's "three times their base movement rate" through familiar areas exactly, with no fractions); when the accrued total reaches 3 × the party's exploration rate, the clock advances one full turn through `EffectsLedger.advance` and the odometer resets. Turn-consuming actions (searching, resting, a `ForceDoor` attempt does **not** — see doors) advance one whole turn and reset the odometer, absorbing the partial move — pinned bookkeeping adaptation. `MoveParty` moves one cell per command, sets facing to the movement direction, rejects into walls and closed doors (`exploration.move.blocked` is a rejection, not an event — moving into a wall is an in-fiction invalid command), rejects while a living member is `entangled` (`cannot_move` — the party doesn't abandon its own implicitly; leaving someone is a game's referee-command decision, pinned), triggers `enter` traps and location-effect hooks, marks the cell explored, and emits area/level `LocationEnteredEvent`s on boundary crossings.
- **Rest cadence.** `turns_since_rest` counts non-rest turns; after six consecutive, a `fatigue` effect (engine-written −1 attack, −1 damage) attaches until a `Rest(turn)` completes — pinned reading of "must rest for one turn every hour" with the penalty from RAW. `Rest(night)` is 48 turns (eight hours, pinned — the SRD has no time-of-day model, so a night is a duration, not a clock position) and marks sleep for the memorization gate. `Rest(day)` is a full 144-turn day; if uninterrupted it applies `natural_healing` per living member (1d3, the Phase 2 function, effects stream) — an interruption (any encounter) voids the healing per RAW's "an interrupted day heals nothing" and ends the rest at the encounter.
- **Memorization gates**, closing the Phase 3 deferral: `PrepareSpells` is legal once per sleep, only after an uninterrupted `Rest(night)`, and costs six turns (one hour) — RAW's three gates, enforced by session state (`slept_since_prepare`); the kernel `memorize_spells` does the validation it always did. Divine and arcane casters gate identically (`Spells.md` applies the preparation rules to both).
- **Provisions.** At each day-boundary crossing the session consumes one ration per living member — standard before iron, pinned (fresh food spoils first; RAW is silent) — and checks water: a carried waterskin satisfies the day, pinned simplification (per-pint bookkeeping is below the simulation floor; registered). Missing food or water increments that member's deprivation counter, emitting `exploration.provisions.short`; the `deprivation_penalties` flag applies work item 4's schedule. In town, provisions consume but never run short (the town sells them).
- **Doors.** `OpenDoor` opens an unstuck, unlocked door (zero time); a stuck door rejects toward `ForceDoor`. `ForceDoor` rolls `open_doors_check` (STR-based X-in-6, exploration stream), costs no turn (forcing is a moment, not a search — pinned; the noise is the cost) and is repeatable. A **failed** attempt denies the party surprise against whatever is beyond that door — RAW, exactly as written; any attempt, success or failure, marks the noise flag for the next wandering check (the banging is identical either way — pinned, registered). Doors the party opened swing shut when the party is no longer in either adjoining cell, unless wedged (`WedgeDoor`, consumes an iron spike) — pinned: RAW's "likely to swing shut" becomes always, making spikes matter. `PickLock` requires thieves' tools, rolls `open_locks` (exploration stream), costs one turn (pinned; RAW is silent and the skill is delicate work, the searching precedent), and on failure locks that thief out of that lock until they gain a level (RAW), tracked in attempt memory. Monsters-open-doors stays prose.
- **Listening.** `ListenAtDoor`: one attempt per character per door, ever — RAW, tracked. The roll (work item 3 chances, exploration stream) is a `DetectionRolledEvent` (referee); the player sees heard-something or silence, and silence is genuinely ambiguous — undead make no noise, so the roll happens regardless of occupants, pinned no-leak convention (the Phase 2 doctrine applied to exploration). Zero time, pinned (RAW gives none; the once-per-door cap is the limiter).
- **Searching.** `Search(kind)` covers the party's current cell, costs one turn, one attempt per character per cell per kind, referee-rolled on the exploration stream whether or not anything is there (no-leak). Success reveals every hidden feature of that kind on the cell (secret doors on its edges → `discovered`; the area room trap if the cell is in the area; construction tricks) — pinned: the 10' × 10' search area is the cell. `InspectTreasure`/`RemoveTreasureTrap` are the thief-only d% pair, one attempt per trap each per RAW, remove-failure consequence pinned: a failed removal springs the trap (the classic reading; RAW says only "attempted once per trap" — registered).
- **Traps.** A trigger action (entering a room-trap area or cell, opening/taking from a trapped cache) rolls the 2-in-6 spring check (exploration stream, referee event); sprung traps resolve their `TrapEffect` — damage automatic, no attack roll, saves per spec, conditions and transitions through the existing kernel — against the triggerer (or party per spec). A found trap no longer springs on movement (the party walks around the known pit — pinned); a sprung one-shot trap stays sprung in state. `TakeTreasure` moves cache/pile contents into inventory (`ItemAcquiredEvent`, the recovered-treasure ledger records coin values for Phase 5).
- **Light.** `LightSource` per work item 2; the party's light state is a query over the ledger — any active `light`/`continual_light`-family effect (torch, lantern, or the Phase 3 spell attachments, whose `radius_feet` params the loop now consumes) on a living member. A `darkness`/`continual_darkness` effect on a member suppresses the whole party's light while it runs (the printed radii swallow a marching party — pinned) and `blocks_infravision: true` disables infravision too, giving the Phase 3 darkness data its promised exploration meaning. Pinned consequences, exactly the RAW set: a lit party cannot surprise monsters (work item 9); `Search`, `InspectTreasure`, `PickLock`, and reading require light or — searching and listening only — infravision (the `infravision` tag or spell effect; infravision cannot read, RAW); `MoveParty` in darkness is legal (stumbling through the dark is a choice) but the party is surprised on 1–3 instead of 1–2, pinned adaptation for the blind party, registered. Light radius beyond the lit/unlit distinction (the 30' figures) ships as data on the effects for front ends; the grid does no ray casting — the spec's "explored/visible flags" ship with visible pinned equal to explored-plus-current-cell, a named simplification of the spec's bullet, registered.
- **Location-bound effects**, closing the Phase 2/3 cell deferrals: an `ActiveEffect.target_ref` may now be a structured cell reference (`cell:{dungeon}:{level}:{x},{y}`, pinned format), and the loop runs enter hooks — a party member entering a cell with location effects triggers each effect's enter behavior in attachment order. Delivered on the mechanism: the **burning-oil pool** (`DropItems` oil + `LightSource` on the party's cell compiles the Phase 2 `burning_oil_pool_definition` onto the cell; entering while it burns deals its 1d8 — "who passes through" is finally the engine's own answer); ***silence* 15' radius's stationary form** (the save-passed outcome now attaches the effect to the target's cell; creatures in that cell cannot cast while there — the Phase 3 registered gap closes, and the party's own cell is checked during battle, whose location is the party's position); ***web*'s blocking cube** (cast at a cell, entering attaches the existing `entangled` escape-countdown effect; the cell cannot be exited until the escape resolves). Re-deferred with the registered manual census: web's flammability (burning one away is game narration via referee release), *cloudkill*'s moving fog and the wall spells (already `manual` casts in the Phase 3 census — the game narrates; nothing regresses).
- **Wandering monsters.** A check fires on every `interval_turns`-th turn crossing outside encounters (cadence suspends in encounter/battle modes and resumes after): chance = `chance_in_six` + 1 if the noise flag was set since the last check + 1 if the party carries a **bright** light (an active light-family effect whose `brightness` is `"daylight"` — *continual light*'s data; RAW modifies for "bright light sources", and the torch/lantern flame is the baseline the printed 1-in-6 already assumes, pinned) − 1 if the party is mid-`Rest` (resting quietly), clamped to [0, 6], skip the roll at 0 — the modifier numbers are pinned for RAW's "referee may increase/decrease" (registered), and the check die draws from the wandering stream. On a hit: d20 on the level's table (wandering stream), `npc_party` rows re-roll until a monster row (draws consumed, pinned — the spec's own Phase 4 note), count dice roll (wandering stream), spawn via `spawn_monster` (monster-spawn stream, ids from the session allocator), encounter begins at 2d6 × 10' (encounter stream). Wandering monsters know the dungeon — they are never surprised by the party's presence but the party may still be surprised, pinned: the monsters come "moving in the direction of the party".

### 9. The encounter procedure — `crawl/encounter.py`

- An encounter (wandering, keyed on area entry, or `SpawnMonsters`) opens with: surprise, distance, reaction. Surprise: each side unaware of the other rolls 1d6 (encounter stream), surprised on 1–2 (party 1–3 in darkness, work item 8); one roll for the whole party, one per monster group. Awareness pins: keyed encounters use the area's `aware` flag for the monsters and the party is unaware unless the encounter was heard (a successful listen at the door marks awareness of that room); the lit-party rule skips the monsters' roll entirely (they cannot be surprised by a light coming); a failed-or-attempted door forcing skips it for that room. Both surprised = no advantage; one side surprised = the other acts for one free round (in battle terms, work item 10; in encounter terms, monsters with a free round and a hostile stance start battle with their surprise round).
- Distance: 2d6 × 10 feet (encounter stream), the initial range-track gap. Reaction: rolled unmodified at encounter start unless the keyed content pins a stance; `Parley` re-rolls with the speaker's `npc_reaction_modifier`, any number of times, each a fresh roll — pinned (RAW invites negotiation and gives no cap; a hostile result self-limits). Stance mapping to default behavior, pinned and registered (RAW's bands assume a referee): 2- attacks now (battle begins); 3–5 hostile — the monsters attack at the end of the next encounter round unless the party has begun evading or improved the stance by parley; 6–8 uncertain — the monsters hold, posture, and re-roll stance next round at +0 (the RAW "confused" beat); 9–11 indifferent — no attack, party may pass, parley, or withdraw freely; 12+ friendly. Encounter mode is round-based: each encounter command is one round beat, monsters act per stance after it; the party may `EngageBattle` at any time (battle begins with the party's initiative advantage only if the monsters were surprised).
- Evasion and pursuit, dungeon procedure per `Evasion_and_Pursuit.md`: `Evade` is legal only before battle begins (RAW). Party rate = slowest living member (running, full feet per round); pursuer rate = the *slowest* rate of the pursuing groups, each group contributing its base ground mode (the mode with no descriptor, else the first printed — flying reads dungeon ceilings, pinned) — slowest-of-pursuers mirrors slowest-of-party, and a pack that strings out is fiction (registered). Faster party: evasion succeeds immediately (`encounter.evasion.succeeded`, encounter ends). Otherwise pursuit resolves round by round on the abstract gap: gap += party rate − pursuer rate each round (both run); `DropItems` of treasure or food during pursuit triggers the distraction roll — 3-in-6 (encounter stream) that the monsters stop, treasure tempting intelligent monsters and food unintelligent ones, where intelligent is pinned as a template whose treasure ref carries letters (hoarders value loot; registered as the proxy, override-correctable per encounter via keyed content). Pursuit ends: distraction success (escaped), gap ≤ 5' (caught: battle at melee), or 30 rounds (both sides spent, monsters give up — pinned terminal; with the party no faster the gap never grows, so the round cap is the escape valve and no sight-loss distance is needed). Every pursuit round advances the clock one round; 30 rounds of running attaches `exhausted` with the RAW −2 attack/damage/AC modifiers until three full turns of rest (`Rest(turn)` × 3 tracked on the effect).
- Encounter conclusion, RAW: an encounter consumes at least one full turn — on `EncounterEndedEvent` the clock advances to the next turn boundary and at minimum one full turn from encounter start, pinned. Defeated, routed, and surrendered monsters post `MonsterDefeatedEvent`s to the ledger; `turned` effects on routed undead release at encounter end, closing the Phase 3 seam.

### 10. The battle state machine — `crawl/battle.py`

- **Shape.** Battle state: the party side plus one or more monster groups, each group with a range-track `distance_feet` from the party (initialized from encounter distance, quantized to feet, closing at encounter rate — printed rate ÷ 3 — per movement phase, stopping at 5', melee reach). Party ranks derive from marching order: rank width is 3 inside a keyed area and 2 in corridor cells under `formation_width_limit` (RAW's "2–3 characters in a 10' passage" pinned to 2, with rooms at 3 — the flag off lifts the cap); ranks recompute from living, unbroken marching order at each round start (the fallen collapse forward). Melee reaches the front rank only, both directions — monsters at 5' engage the front rank, and rear ranks use missiles and spells over their fellows' heads (RAW is silent; pinned, no firing-into-melee penalty exists in the SRD). Monster groups array at the same width for footprint math.
- **The round**, exactly the SRD sequence: `ResolveBattleRound(declarations)` carries one `BattleDeclaration` per living, able party member — attack (target group, weapon), cast (spell, mode, targets), turn undead (resolving in the magic phase but undeclared-in-advance and never disruptable — turning is a class ability, not a spell, and the RAW declaration step names spells and melee movement only, pinned), move (close/withdraw/fighting-withdrawal/retreat), use item, or hold. Validation is the pure pre-phase: every declaration validates (kernel validators plus range/rank legality) or the whole command rejects listing every rejection — pinned: partial acceptance would tangle the replay contract. Resolution: spell and melee-movement declarations post (`SpellDeclaredEvent`); side initiative (combat stream, individual under the flag); then per side in initiative order — monster morale (auto-invoked, below), movement (range track, withdrawal at half, retreat at full with the +2/no-shield exposure), missiles (range-band modifiers off the track, reload bookkeeping from the machine's own round memory — the flag's promised honest context), magic (casts resolve via `cast_spell` with `CastContext.in_combat=True`, distance from the track), melee. Slow-weapon actors act last per the Phase 2 ordering. Each battle round advances the clock one round through the ledger — regeneration, splash douses, and spell durations tick mid-battle by construction.
- **Disruption, detected at last**: a caster whose declaration is a spell and who is successfully attacked or fails a saving throw after initiative resolves against them but before their action loses the spell via `disrupt_casting` — the machine tracks act order and applies the RAW trigger the Phase 3 goldens scripted by hand. Concentration effects release when their caster takes any other declared action or is disrupted, pinned.
- **Phase 3 effect consumption**, the promised census, all machine-side: *haste* (`attacks_multiplier` resolves the bearer's attack declaration twice; `movement_multiplier` doubles track moves); *invisibility* (invisible combatants are excluded from declaration targeting and from the default policy's target pool — a rejection here leaks nothing, you know what you can't see — and the machine releases the effect when the bearer attacks or turns undead, completing the casting-side break Phase 3 wired); *mirror image* (each incoming attack against the bearer pops an image via the Phase 3 `pop_mirror_image` instead of resolving, until images exhaust); *confusion* (a confused actor's declaration is overridden by the 2d6 behavior roll from its structured params, drawn from the combat stream — a forced rules roll, not a policy choice — and the above-2-HD per-round re-save runs on the magic stream per its params); *afraid* and *turned* (the group or member flees: forced retreat moves until clear of the battle, the encounter releasing `turned` at its end per work item 9); `entangled` (`cannot_move` blocks declared and policy moves; an entangled front-ranker still fights); `weakened` (cannot attack or cast — the kernel validators already reject, the machine surfaces them); the *protection from evil* melee ban (`bars_melee_from` params: a monster whose template bears the `enchanted` category may not initiate melee against a warded target whose alignment differs — the default policy's target pick skips them; missiles and breath are unaffected, and the ward's 10' radius form covers exactly the targets Phase 3 attached it to — pinned).
- **The monster action policy**: `ActionPolicy` protocol — `choose(group, battle, stream) -> group actions` — pluggable per encounter side, default `ScriptedPolicy`, drawing only from the new `"monster_action"` stream so a policy change never shifts attack or damage draws, pinned. Default behavior, pinned: monsters with a scripted pattern in their data follow it — dragons open with breath, then breath or melee with equal chance while daily uses remain (RAW); the hellhound's 2-in-6 per-round breath gate rolls each round; otherwise a group beyond 5' with a usable ranged form (breath, missile routine) uses it, else closes; at 5' it melees. Each attacking monster picks its target uniformly (monster-action stream) from the reachable rank; helpless targets resolve by the Phase 2 auto-hit rules. Groups never cast — casting tags are `manual` (work item: out of scope) — and a substituted policy or LLM referee may do anything the kernel validates.
- **Morale, auto-invoked**: the machine calls the Phase 2 `morale_triggers` helper after each side's action block — first death on a side, half the side incapacitated — checking through a per-battle `MoraleTracker` with the group's data score, conditional alternates chosen by context (fear-of-fire when the round's damage included fire, pinned example), and `morale_modifier` folded per the Phase 3 rule. ML 2 groups rout when battle starts, RAW. Failed morale = the group flees: retreat exposure that round, then the group moves away at full speed and leaves the battle when its gap exceeds 120' (the pursuit sight figure, symmetric — pinned); the party may let them go or the battle ends into a pursuit the *party* initiates — out: chasing routed monsters is an evasion procedure with roles swapped, deferred as game territory, registered. Surrender (the reaction table's and subdual's domain) stays a stance outcome, not a machine state, in 1.0.
- **Areas against the combat space**, closing the Phase 2/3 geometry deferral: an area effect (spell or breath) targets one group; its capacity in creatures is `ceil(span / 10) × width`, where span is the diameter (radius shapes), length (lines, clouds), or reach-limited length (cones: length minus the gap), and width is the formation width — pinned deterministic footprint, registered. Candidates fill in stable spawn order up to capacity; with `aoe_friendly_fire` on, an area landing on a group at melee range (≤ 5') appends the engaged party front rank after the monsters, in marching order, consuming remaining capacity — the documented adaptation. Breath weapons against the party cover `ceil(reach-limited span / 10)` ranks from the front. Gaze runs per the Phase 2 kernel against engaged combatants.
- **Ends.** Victory (all groups dead/fled/surrendered) posts per-monster `MonsterDefeatedEvent`s and returns to the encounter conclusion; party flight (`move: retreat` declarations from every member) converts to a pursuit with the current minimum gap; TPK sets `game_over`. The XP inputs (defeated monsters with template ids and XP values) land in the adventure ledger for Phase 5.

### 11. GameSession, views, persistence — `crawl/session.py`, `osrlib/persistence.py`

- `GameSession.new(party, adventure, *, seed, ruleset)` owns what the kernel has been promising the session would own: the `RngStreams` (master seed), the `IdAllocator`, the `EffectsLedger`, the `GameClock`, the entity registry (characters and live monster instances), the flag store, the listener-state store, the command and event logs, the mode, and the crawl state (dungeon state, odometer, cadence counters, attempt memory, ledgers, death records). Death records close the Phase 3 attestation seam, pinned: when a character dies the session records the clock round and the cause it just resolved (`poison` when the killing resolution was a poison save or poison-delay expiry, else the source kind); a `CastSpell` of *neutralize poison* or *raise dead* populates `CastContext.days_since_death` from the record and the clock always, and `rounds_since_death` only when the recorded cause is poison — the kernel's window checks then run on honest numbers instead of caller attestations. `session.metadata` exposes `schema_version` and `engine_version` for the spec's front-end handshake. `execute(command) -> CommandResult`: parse-validated commands run the pure validation pre-phase (a rejected command consumes no draws, no time, mutates nothing, and is excluded from the command log — the Phase 0 contract, now enforced end to end); accepted commands mutate, append their events to the log, then listeners run in registration order, their events appended to the same result and log. New stream constants: `WANDERING_STREAM = "wandering"`, `ENCOUNTER_STREAM = "encounter"`, `EXPLORATION_STREAM = "exploration"`, `MONSTER_ACTION_STREAM = "monster_action"` — scoped exactly as the work items above assign draws, so a search-rule change never shifts an encounter golden, pinned.
- Listeners: `register_listener(listener)` where a listener exposes `key: str` and `handle(events, state: dict) -> tuple[list[Event], dict]`; state snapshots into saves under the key; listeners never mutate game state (they react by executing ordinary commands, the spec's quest-tracker contract). Games may define their own `Event` subclasses; the session log accepts any `Event`, and on load, log entries whose types this process doesn't know are preserved as raw records that reserialize losslessly — pinned: the log is a record, never re-derived, never lossy.
- Views: `view(Visibility.PLAYER) -> PlayerView`, a frozen whitelist projection, pinned field census: party (public sheet: HP, conditions, inventory, memorized spells), location and facing, explored cells with their edges (secret doors only if discovered), known dropped piles and emptied caches in explored space, active effects on party members with remaining durations (players track their own torches and spells in B/X), elapsed clock, mode, current encounter/battle public state (monster names, counts, distances, visible conditions — never HP), fatigue/exhaustion/deprivation status, and the adventure's public prose (name, description, town). Never present, locked by test: unexplored geometry, undiscovered traps or secret doors, monster HP or stat internals, referee-visibility roll outcomes, session flags (referee-only, pinned — content wiring is the game's secret), RNG state, or the seed (the seed lives only in the save — pinned: neither view carries it). `RefereeView` is the full state projection minus RNG internals.
- `osrlib/persistence.py`: `save_game(session, *, include_event_log=True) -> dict` — a `stamp_document("save", ...)` envelope carrying the full state (party, adventure content embedded — saves are self-contained, pinned — dungeon state, clock, ledger, allocator, registry monsters, flags, listener state, mode, crawl counters, exported RNG stream states, the master seed, the accepted-command log always, the event log optionally). `load_game(document) -> GameSession` runs `check_document`, then the ordered migration chain — `MIGRATIONS: dict[int, Callable]`, empty at schema 1; the framework is exercised by a test with a synthetic in-test migration — then restores streams via `RngStream.restore`. `replay_game(seed, party_doc, adventure, ruleset, commands) -> GameSession` re-executes; a command log recorded under a different engine version raises the new `ReplayVersionError(OsrlibError)` — the spec's explicit, detectable error (loading a *save* across engine versions remains legal; replay is the guarantee that breaks). The standing test: `load(save)` equals `replay(seed, commands)` for every Phase 4 golden, at every checkpoint.
