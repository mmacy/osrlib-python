# Phase 3 plan — magic

Implementation plan for Phase 3 of [the osrlib spec](spec.md). Phase 3 delivers the spell compiler (the 106 SRD spell pages behind the cleric and magic-user lists, with structured mechanics for the automatable subset), spell memorization and casting for divine and arcane casters, spell disruption, turning undead, and spell durations riding the Phase 2 effects engine. It also closes the Phase 1 character-creation seam (the magic-user's and elf's starting spell book) and the Phase 2 forward references (*lightning bolt* as a destructive death, the *sleep* arithmetic the HD-budget resolver deferred, *cure* spells consuming the healing and mummy-rot hooks, *stone to flesh* recovering the petrified). Milestone from the spec: cleric and magic-user play through combat using spells, including a disrupted casting, a *sleep* resolved by HD budget, and a *fire ball* resolved per-target against an explicitly supplied target list (geometry-to-target mapping arrives with Phase 4's combat space).

## Scope

In scope:

- `tools/srd_compile/spells.py` — the spell compiler, with `overrides/spells.json` for parse corrections; `tools/srd_compile/combat_tables.py` grows the turning-undead table (source `Cleric.md`); `tools/srd_compile/classes.py` enriches the `divine_magic`/`arcane_magic` tags with their spell-list params
- `src/osrlib/data/` — generated `spells.json` with typed loader `load_spells()`; `combat_tables.json` gains the turning table
- `core/spells.py` — the new module: frozen `SpellTemplate` (presentation data plus structured modes and effects), memorization, casting, disruption, turning undead, and the `"magic"` RNG stream
- `core/tables.py` — turning-table lookup
- `core/character.py` — additive `spell_book` and `memorized_spells` fields; the starting-spells creation step
- `core/classes.py` — the drain/memorization interplay (excess memorized spells forgotten when slots shrink)
- `core/effects.py` — new conditions with combat hooks, the engine-written stat-modifier machinery, spell tick/expiry handlers, dispel plumbing (`dispellable`, `caster_level`)
- `core/combat.py` — consultation of engine-written modifiers in attack/damage/save/morale resolution; the nonmagical-missile immunity gate; per-die elemental damage reduction
- `core/events.py` and `osrlib/messages.py` — the magic event set and its templates
- Tests: data pipeline and turning-table fidelity, memorization/casting/disruption/turning suites, modifier and condition suites, property tests, and the milestone goldens

Out of scope (deferred to the phase that consumes them):

- Scrolls, wands, staves, potions, and all magic-item casting (5) — `Scrolls_and_Maps.md` is a treasure page; the thief's `scroll_use` tag stays a tag, and the magic-user's "written in magical script" rule rides with scroll items
- Geometry-to-target mapping for areas (4) — every cast in Phase 3 resolves against an explicitly supplied target list, per the spec's milestone note; area shapes and dimensions ship as structured data now
- The battle machine's spell-declaration tracking and disruption *trigger detection* (4) — the kernel resolves a disruption when told it happened; detecting "lost initiative and was hit or failed a save before acting" is the round loop's job. Same for concentration-break detection: concentration effects are released by the caller
- Location-bound area effects on cells (4) — *web*'s 10' cube blocking, *cloudkill*'s moving fog, *silence*'s stationary form, the wall spells; the on-creature forms land now, and `target_ref` location strings exist for games that want them early
- Illumination and vision rules (4) — the light/darkness/infravision spells attach structured effects now; the exploration loop's light rules consume them
- Rest and timing gates on memorization (4) — "once a day, after an uninterrupted night's sleep, takes one hour" is exploration-loop procedure; the kernel exposes the preparation operation and the crawl layer decides when it may run
- Monster and NPC spellcasting (4/5) — monster casting tags stay `manual` data for Phase 4's action policy; NPC adventuring parties cast through the same character model when Phase 5 builds them
- Spell-book growth costs (mentoring's week, rewriting's 1,000 gp per level) — magical research is out of 1.0 scope; `add_spell_to_book` ships as the validated referee-level surface and games own the fiction around it
- Deity disfavour and the alignment norms for reversed divine spells — referee judgment with no mechanics ("The referee must determine appropriate measures"); ships as prose only
- The `magic_item_death_save` flag (5) — Phase 3 adds **no** Ruleset flags: the spec's 1.0 flag set contains no magic-owned entries, and every choice below is either rules-as-written or a pinned interpretation, not a referee-optional variant

## Work items

### 1. Spell data model — `core/spells.py`

- `SpellTemplate` (frozen, `overrides_applied` like every compiled model): `id`, `name`, `spell_list` (`cleric` | `magic_user`), `level`, `duration` (raw string plus structured `DurationSpec`), `range` (raw plus `RangeSpec`), `reversed_form: ReversedForm | None` (name, prose, its own modes — reversed forms are **not** separate catalog entries), `modes: tuple[SpellMode, ...]`, intro prose.
- IDs are the slugified primary spell name: `fire_ball`, `sleep`, `cure_light_wounds` (the reversed parenthetical is dropped from the id — the reverse lives on the entry). The nine concepts with separate `(C)` and `(MU)` pages compile as **two entries** with `_c`/`_mu` suffixes (`hold_person_c`, `hold_person_mu`, `light_c`, `light_mu`, `continual_light_c`/`_mu`, `detect_evil_c`/`_mu`, `detect_magic_c`/`_mu`, `locate_object_c`/`_mu`, `protection_from_evil_c`/`_mu`, `protection_from_evil_10_radius_c`/`_mu`, `remove_curse_c`/`_mu`) — pinned, because the pairs differ mechanically (Hold Person is 2nd-level cleric at 9 turns/180' but 3rd-level magic-user at 1 turn per level/120'), so one merged entry would misstate at least one class's spell. The `invisible_stalker` spell id coexists with the monster id of the same name — catalog ids are unique per catalog, noted in both docstrings.
- `DurationSpec`: `kind` ∈ {`instant`, `permanent`, `concentration`, `fixed`, `special`} with `unit` (`rounds`/`turns`/`days`), `amount`, `dice`, `per_level` (the additive bonus: *light (MU)* is 6 turns +1 per level), and `concentration_cap`. Dual-form durations (`Instant / Permanent (curse)`) split across the normal and reversed forms. Anything unparseable keeps `kind="special"` with the raw string — the parser never fails on prose.
- `RangeSpec`: `kind` ∈ {`caster`, `touch`, `feet`, `yards`, `per_level`, `special`} plus `feet`. `The caster or a creature touched` is `touch` (self-targeting allowed); `240 yards around the caster` is `yards`.
- `SpellMode` — one castable usage (multi-usage pages like *cure light wounds* and *light* carry one mode per numbered usage): `key` (stable snake_case within the spell), `targeting` (a `TargetingSpec` naming the Phase 2 `TargetingMode` plus its params: `count`, `count_dice` — *hold person*'s group mode is 1d4, `hd_budget_dice` — *sleep*'s 2d8, `hd_cap`, area `shape`/`dimensions`), `save` (`SaveCategory`, modifier — *hold person*'s single-target −2, *feeblemind*'s −4, and `on_save` ∈ {`negates`, `half`}), `effect: SpellEffect | None`, `manual: bool`, prose.
- `SpellEffect` — the closed vocabulary the casting interpreter executes; params pinned per spell in work item 7: `damage` (dice, dice-per-caster-level, element, `destructive`, auto-hit, touch-attack), `heal` (dice, what it cures), `cure` (conditions/effects removed), `condition` (which `Condition`, duration override), `modifiers` (the stat-modifier bundle of work item 6), `kill` (save-or-die with restrictions), `restore_life`, `dispel`, `attach_only` (structured effect attached; consumption arrives later or belongs to the game).
- Conjured-creature stat blocks embedded in spell pages ship structured: *sticks to snakes* carries its snake as a full inline `MonsterTemplate` (validated by the Phase 2 model, id `conjured_snake`, XP 10 with the poisonous variant as an override-documented note); *conjure elemental* references the existing `air/earth/fire/water_elemental_greater` ids (16 HD — the compiler asserts the references resolve against `monsters.json`). Summon *lifecycles* (obedience, dismissal, loss of control) are `manual` prose — the kernel ships the template and `spawn_monster` already exists; wiring them is one game call.
- `MAGIC_STREAM = "magic"` (module constant, following the `COMBAT_STREAM` precedent): every draw inside spell resolution — targeting dice, damage dice, touch-attack rolls, forced saving throws, charm re-save scheduling, dispel survival rolls, and both turning rolls — comes from the caster's `"magic"` stream, so a combat-rules change never shifts spell goldens and vice versa. Effect-internal draws (rolled durations at attach, *web* escape dice) stay on the `"effects"` stream per the Phase 2 convention.

### 2. SRD compiler — `tools/srd_compile/spells.py` and the turning table

Same shape as the prior parsers: `compile_spells(srd_dir)` slots into `__main__.py`, validates through the shipped models, writes with the established deterministic conventions.

- Page inventory: the manifest is the two list pages — `Cleric_Spells.md` (34 entries over levels 1–5) and `Magic-User_Spells.md` (72 entries, 12 per level over levels 1–6) — read as the authority, exactly like `Monster_Descriptions.md` was for monsters. 34 + 72 = 106 pages, mapping 1:1 to the files that carry the italic level line; the elf has no list of its own (`Elf.md`: uses the magic-user list).
- Spell pages are identified by the italic level line `*<N>(st|nd|rd|th) Level [<Class> Spell](...)*` — **never** by the `**Duration:**`/`**Range:**` labels, which also appear on `Potions.md`, `Hazards_and_Challenges.md`, `Water_Vessels.md`, and monster/magic-item pages and would false-positive a label scanner. The level line is the only place level and class are encoded.
- Page structure is uniform: H1 title (with the reversed name and/or `(C)`/`(MU)` marker parenthesized), the level line, `**Duration:**`, `**Range:**`, free prose with `- **Lead-in:**` bullets and numbered usage lists, and reversible spells carrying a trailing `## Reversed: <Name>` H2 (exactly 16 pages).
- Parse hazards found during survey, recorded so the implementer doesn't rediscover them:
    - Filename encoding is inconsistent: parentheses are single-encoded (`%28`/`%29`) but the typographic foot mark `’` (U+2019) is **double**-encoded on disk (`Silence_15%25E2%2580%2599_Radius.md`, `Invisibility_10%25E2%2580%2599_Radius.md`, and the two `Protection_from_Evil_10%25E2%2580%2599_Radius_%28C/MU%29.md` files, which mix both schemes in one name). The list-page hrefs use *single* encoding for the same files, so the href→file resolver must re-encode `%` → `%25`.
    - `’` (U+2019) is the foot mark and apostrophe throughout stat lines and prose (`240’`, `20’ radius`, `caster’s`); `×` (U+00D7) appears in embedded stat blocks; en-dashes in ranges (`INT 3–8`, `6th–10th`); `—` (U+2014) as the turning table's empty cell.
    - Duration values range from keywords (`Instant`, `Permanent`, `Concentration`) through dice (`4d4 turns`, `2d6 turns`) and per-level arithmetic (`6 turns +1 per level`, `1 turn per level`, `1d6 turns +1 per level`) to pure prose (`One mission`, `Until touched`, `Permanent (but may be broken, see below)`) and slash-separated dual forms (`Instant / Permanent (*curse*)` — italics inconsistent between files). *Charm person*/*charm monster* put a full rules paragraph inside a second `**Duration:**` label deeper in the page — the parser takes the first.
    - Range values include feet, `240 yards around the caster`, `150–300 yards.` (trailing period), `The caster`, `The caster or a creature touched`, `The caster or a creature or object touched`, and `10’ around the caster`. No page uses a numeric zero for self-range.
    - `Sticks_to_Snakes.md` embeds a full monster stat line between horizontal rules (`**AC** 6 [13], **HD** 1 (4hp), …`) — parsed with the Phase 2 stat-line machinery, not skipped as prose. `Conjure_Elemental.md` embeds four such blocks (matched against `monsters.json` instead of recompiled).
    - `Spells.md` and `Rules_of_Magic.md` are byte-identical duplicates (the latter ends `Note: entry included from Spells.`) — `Spells.md` is pinned as the single rules source; the compiler never reads `Rules_of_Magic.md`.
    - Reversibility is marked by parentheses in titles and list entries, never asterisks — asterisks in this neighborhood mean HD special-ability counts (`2*` in the turning table) and CON footnotes (`9d6+1*`), not reversal.
- Structured mechanics are hand-curated in the compiler, not parsed from prose: a `_SPELL_MECHANICS` table in `tools/srd_compile/spells.py` maps spell id → modes/targeting/save/effect params, following the `_CLASS_ABILITIES` precedent from the Phase 1 class compiler. Prose is always shipped alongside; `overrides/spells.json` stays reserved for corrections on top of parsing, with `overrides_applied` provenance. The compiler fails if a `_SPELL_MECHANICS` id doesn't resolve to a parsed page.
- Census validations at compile time: 106 pages, 34 + 72 list entries all resolving to files, 16 reversed sections matching the 16 parenthesized-title list entries, 9 dual-page concepts, every mode's `Condition`/`SaveCategory`/`TargetingMode` value valid.
- `compile_combat_tables` grows the turning table from `Cleric.md` (added to `SOURCE_PAGES`): 11 cleric-level rows (1–10, `11+`) × 8 monster-HD columns (`1`, `2`, `2*`, `3`, `4`, `5`, `6`, `7-9`), cells ∈ {`—`, number, `T`, `D`}, asserted verbatim by a fidelity test.
- `compile_classes` enriches the existing empty-param caster tags: `divine_magic` gains `{"spell_list": "cleric"}`, `arcane_magic` gains `{"spell_list": "magic_user"}` (magic-user and elf both) — additive params inside existing tags, no new tags, closing the survey finding that the elf's caster nature is only prose today.

### 3. Generated data, loaders — `src/osrlib/data/`

- `spells.json` with the `_meta` block (`source_pages`: the 106 spell pages plus the two list pages); `combat_tables.json` regenerates with the turning table and `Cleric.md` in its `_meta`. Never hand-edited; the existing CI regeneration diff covers both automatically.
- `load_spells() -> SpellCatalog` in `osrlib/data/__init__.py`, following the exact Phase 1/2 loader pattern (`importlib.resources`, `@cache`, frozen models, `ContentValidationError` on validation failure, id-uniqueness validator, `.get(id)` raising `ValueError`). `SpellCatalog` also exposes `by_list(spell_list, level)` for memorization validation and creation choices.
- `tests/test_srd_data.py`'s `DATA_FILES` tuple grows `spells.json`.

### 4. Events and the message formatter — `core/events.py`, `osrlib/messages.py`

New frozen `Event` subclasses join the discriminated union additively (new `event_type` literals, `SCHEMA_VERSION` stays 1); every code gets a `messages.py` template, locked by the existing exhaustive test:

- `SpellsMemorizedEvent` (`magic.memorize.prepared`, player) — one event per preparation, carrying the full prepared list (spell ids + reversed flags).
- `SpellCastEvent` (`magic.cast.cast` / `magic.cast.no_effect`, player) — caster, spell id, mode, reversed flag, target ids, and a `manual` marker on modes the kernel doesn't execute (the game narrates those). Resolution consequences ride the existing event types (`SavingThrowRolledEvent`, `DamageDealtEvent`, `ConditionGainedEvent`, `EffectAttachedEvent`, `HealingAppliedEvent`, `DeathEvent`, ...), exactly as breath weapons do.
- `SpellDisruptedEvent` (`magic.cast.disrupted`, player) — the copy lost to disruption.
- `SpellForgottenEvent` (`magic.memory.forgotten`, player) — drain-shrunk slots (work item 5).
- `SpellBookUpdatedEvent` (`magic.book.added`, player).
- `UndeadTurnedEvent` (`magic.turning.turned` / `.destroyed` / `.failed`, player — the player rolls turning dice in B/X) — the 2d6 turn roll, the 2d6 HD pool when rolled, and per-type outcomes; per-monster consequences ride `ConditionGainedEvent`/`DeathEvent`.
- `MagicDispelledEvent` (`magic.dispel.resolved`, player) — released effect ids and survivors.

### 5. Character, creation, memorization — `core/character.py`, `core/classes.py`, `core/spells.py`

- `Character` grows two additive fields (the slot `character.py` reserved in Phase 1): `spell_book: tuple[str, ...] = ()` (spell ids; arcane casters only) and `memorized_spells: tuple[MemorizedSpell, ...] = ()` where `MemorizedSpell` (frozen) is `spell_id` + `reversed: bool`. Tuple order is memorization order and is load-bearing (consumption and forgetting rules below). Spell slots themselves stay derived — `definition.row(level).spell_slots`, never stored — so leveling and drain recompute them for free, as Phase 1 designed.
- `memorize_spells(character, definition, catalog, selections) -> MemorizationResult` — full replacement, modeling the daily preparation: validators check the caster has a `divine_magic`/`arcane_magic` tag, per-spell-level counts fit `row(level).spell_slots`, every spell belongs to the class's `spell_list` at a level the character has slots for, divine selections never set `reversed` (divine casters reverse at cast time — `Spells.md`: "by speaking the words and performing the gestures backwards when it is cast"), arcane selections come from `spell_book` and may set `reversed` only on reversible spells (`Spells.md`: "The normal or reversed form of a spell must be selected when the spell is memorized"). Duplicate selections are legal per RAW ("may opt to memorize the same spell twice"). Rejection codes are dotted snake_case (`magic.memorize.not_a_caster`, `.slots_exceeded`, `.not_in_book`, `.wrong_list`, ...), shaped for Phase 4 command rejections. The once-a-day/after-sleep/one-hour gates are Phase 4's; standalone users call it freely — pinned.
- Starting spells, closing the Phase 1 seam: creation grows `choose_starting_spells(character, definition, catalog, spell_ids)` — arcane casters begin with a book holding exactly their memorization capacity (`Spell_Books.md`: "begin play with as many spells in their spell book as they are able to memorize"), which at level 1 is one first-level spell for both magic-user and elf; the caller supplies the choice ("The referee may choose these spells or may allow the player to select" — the game owns the decision, the kernel validates it). Clerics start with nothing: the level-1 progression row has no slots ("Once a cleric has proven their faith (from 2nd level), the character may pray to receive spells"). `create_character(...)` grows the step in SRD order; creation stays event-less per the Phase 1 precedent.
- `add_spell_to_book(character, definition, catalog, spell_id) -> result` — the referee-level growth surface for mentoring/leveling: validates arcane caster, list membership, and per-level capacity (pinned: book capacity per spell level equals the current slot count at that level, the RAW "contains exactly the number of spells that the character is capable of memorizing" read per level). Emits `magic.book.added`.
- Drain interplay in `drain_levels`: after the level drops, for each spell level where the memorized count now exceeds the new slot count, excess copies are forgotten newest-first (highest tuple index) with `SpellForgottenEvent`s — pinned; RAW is silent and newest-first keeps the rule deterministic without new state. The spell *book* never auto-shrinks — it is a physical object; a drained character may hold a book over capacity and simply cannot add more until capacity catches up — pinned.
- Holy symbol, pinned: **not** enforced as a casting or turning precondition. `Cleric.md` states "A cleric must carry a holy symbol" as a class edict alongside deity faithfulness, not as a mechanical gate on any procedure; the item ships in the Phase 1 gear data and games that want the stricter reading can check inventory themselves. Register entry names the consequence as chosen.
- Casting-incapacity conditions: `validate_cast` rejects a caster who is dead, petrified, paralysed, asleep, silenced, feebleminded, or under raise-dead weakness; `bound`/`gagged` arrive as caller context flags (`Combat.md` Freedom rule) since the kernel has no restraint model.

### 6. Effects engine extensions — `core/effects.py`, consulted by `core/combat.py`

- New `Condition` members (additive vocabulary, wire values pinned lowercase): `silenced`, `entangled`, `afraid`, `feebleminded`, `invisible`, `turned`, `confused`, `weakened` (the raise-dead recovery state). Combat hooks wired this phase: `silenced`/`feebleminded`/`weakened` cannot cast (validator); `weakened` cannot attack; `entangled` cannot move (movement is Phase 4 — the hook ships as a queryable predicate like `incapacitated`); `afraid`/`turned`/`confused`/`invisible` are marker states consumed by Phase 4's battle machine and by games, documented as such.
- Stat modifiers — the machinery every buff/debuff spell needs, mirroring the Phase 2 condition pattern exactly: `Character` and `MonsterInstance` grow `stat_modifiers: tuple[ActiveModifier, ...]` (additive field), where `ActiveModifier` (frozen) is `kind`, `value`, scope params, and the owning `effect_id`. **Only the effects engine writes it** (attach grants, expiry/release removes — the single-writer rule extends; the desync invariant test extends to cover it). Combat reads it locally, so `attack_roll`/`damage_roll`/`saving_throw`/`check_morale` signatures don't grow a ledger parameter.
- Modifier kinds pinned this phase: `attack_bonus`, `damage_bonus`, `morale_bonus` (*bless*/*blight*), `save_bonus` (scoped: unconditional, per element — *resist cold/fire*, or versus creatures of another alignment — *protection from evil*), `ac_set` and `ac_set_vs_missile` (*shield*), `attack_penalty_of_attackers` (*protection from evil*'s −1, applied when `attack_roll` sees a warded defender and the attacker's alignment differs), `damage_reduction_per_die` (element-scoped; `deal_damage` already receives per-die rolls, and each die floors at 1 — *resist cold/fire*), `missile_immunity_nonmagical` (*protection from normal missiles*: `DamageSource.kind == "missile"` and not magical resolves as no effect), `melee_damage_dice_bonus` + `counts_as_magical` (*striking*).
- Cumulative-effects rule, pinned: `Spells.md` says "Multiple spells affecting the same game statistic do not combine." At consultation time, per statistic, only the single largest bonus and the single largest penalty apply (a *bless* and a *blight* offset; two *blesses* don't stack). Spell effects combine freely with non-spell modifiers (the RAW carve-out for magic items, which arrive in Phase 5).
- AC-set semantics pinned: *shield* grants "AC 2 [17] against missiles, AC 4 [15] against other attacks" — the target's effective AC is the better of their own and the set value, never worse (a plate-armoured target keeps plate).
- Dispel plumbing: `EffectDefinition` gains `dispellable: bool = False`; `ActiveEffect` gains `caster_level: int | None = None`. Every effect attached by `cast_spell` sets `dispellable=True` and records the caster's level; monster-inflicted effects (poison, rot, regeneration) stay non-dispellable. Pinned: *all* spell-attached effects are dispellable, including permanent ones (*continual light*, *flesh to stone*) — RAW says "ends spell effects", full stop; `permanent=True` on an effect means "no duration expiry", not "undispellable".
- Per-level durations are computed at cast: `cast_spell` builds the (frozen) `EffectDefinition` per cast with `duration_amount` resolved from the caster's level; dice durations keep the Phase 2 behavior (rolled at attach from the `"effects"` stream).
- New tick/expiry handlers dispatched by the existing string registry: `charm_resave` (periodic saving throw at the INT-banded interval; a passed save releases the effect), `delayed_death` variants reused for *cause disease*'s 2d12-day death, and the *web* escape countdown. The Phase 2 tick order, suspension, and single-writer invariants are untouched.

### 7. Casting, disruption, and the automated census — `core/spells.py`

- `validate_cast(caster, spell, mode, *, reversed, targets, context) -> list[Rejection]` — the pure pre-phase: caster capacity conditions (work item 5), a matching memorized copy (arcane: form must match; divine: any copy of the spell — the reversed flag is free at cast), target-count and mode legality (single takes one, group modes take a candidate list), reversible-form legality. No RNG, no mutation. Range is validated only when `context` supplies a distance, mirroring the combat convention.
- Category and immunity gates are **resolution** outcomes, never validator rejections — pinned, extending the Phase 2 holy-water doctrine: rejections are free and would leak hidden state (casting *charm person* at a disguised doppelgänger or *sleep* at a wight must not be a zero-cost undead detector). Casting at an ineligible target consumes the spell and reports `magic.cast.no_effect` (or that target simply isn't affected in group modes).
- `cast_spell(caster, spell, mode, *, reversed=False, targets, ledger, clock, allocator, registry, ruleset, stream) -> CastResult` — consumes the first matching memorized copy (lowest tuple index), resolves the mode, and returns the events. Manual modes (`manual=True`) consume the copy and emit `magic.cast.cast` with the manual marker plus the prose — the kernel does the bookkeeping, the game narrates the effect; that is the à la carte contract for the non-automated majority. Casting anything releases the caster's own `invisible` effect (RAW: attacking or casting breaks invisibility).
- `disrupt_casting(caster, spell_id, *, reversed) -> list[Event]` — removes the copy "as if it had been cast" (`Combat.md`) and emits `magic.cast.disrupted`. The trigger — lost initiative, then successfully attacked or failed a save before acting — is detected by the Phase 4 battle machine; the milestone golden scripts the RAW trigger by hand.
- Touch-attack modes (*cause light/serious wounds*): when the context marks combat, the kernel rolls the melee attack internally (attack matrix, caster's THAC0, 1d20 from the `"magic"` stream); the copy is consumed whether or not the touch lands — pinned (nothing in RAW holds the charge). Out of combat the touch simply lands ("In combat, a melee attack roll is required" — the inverse is pinned: no roll outside combat).
- Spell saving throws always pass `magical=True` (the WIS modifier applies, per Phase 2's pin) and carry the mode's save modifier and element; energy `auto_save` defenses (red dragon versus *fire ball*) and graded immunities resolve through the existing `saving_throw`/`check_immunity`/`deal_damage` pipeline unchanged.
- The automated census — every mode below is `manual=False` and fully resolved by the interpreter; parameters are pinned here from the SRD pages and locked by tests. Everything not listed ships `manual=True` with prose (and structured extras where noted).
    - **Damage and save-or-die.** *Magic missile*: auto-hit (no attack roll, no save), 1d6+1 per missile, missile count = 1 + 2 × ⌊(caster level − 1) / 5⌋ (pinned from "three missiles at 6th–10th level, five at 11th–15th"), multiple missiles may stack on one target, resolved instantly at cast (the 1-turn duration is holding prose, pinned). *Fire ball*: 1d6 × caster level, element fire, save versus spells for half (halving floors, Phase 2 pin). *Lightning bolt*: identical arithmetic, and its `DamageSource` is `destructive=True` — a death by *lightning bolt* destroys equipment, closing the Phase 2 forward reference; the bounce-back is `manual` prose. *Death spell*: 4d8 HD budget through the Phase 2 `hd_budget` resolver against supplied candidates, excluding undead and creatures over 7 HD; each affected creature saves versus death or dies. *Finger of death* (reversed *raise dead*): single target, save versus death or dies. *Disintegrate*: single non-magical creature, save versus death or destroyed — pinned as permanent death (`kill(permanent=True)`) plus equipment destruction (the material form is destroyed).
    - **Sleep**, closing the Phase 2 deferral: no saving throw; undead are excluded (their `asleep` condition immunity already encodes it) and consume no budget — exclusion happens in resolution, not selection, per the no-leak doctrine. Mode 1: a single creature "with 4+1 Hit Dice" — pinned as HD count 4 with a positive fixed modifier. Mode 2: 2d8 HD of creatures of 4 HD or less — eligibility is HD count ≤ 4 excluding count-4-with-positive-modifier (those are mode 1's targets); cost per creature is the HD count with fixed bonuses dropped (the page's own "3+2 counts as 3" rule) and sub-1 HD rounded up to 1; weakest-first with stable-order ties, whole creatures only, budget spends per the Phase 2 resolver. Affected creatures gain `asleep` for 4d4 turns; the Phase 2 asleep hooks (auto-hit, dies to a blade) do the rest.
    - **Hold and charm.** *Hold person* (both entries) and *hold monster*: mode 1 single target saves versus spells at −2; mode 2 targets 1d4 individuals from the candidate group, each saving unmodified; failure attaches `paralysed` for the spell's duration (cleric 9 turns; magic-user 1 turn per level; *hold monster* 6 turns +1 per level). *Hold person* affects only persons — pinned gate: any `Character`, or a `MonsterInstance` bearing the `person` category; undead never (immunity resolves as no effect). *Hold monster* affects anything but undead. *Charm person*: single person-gated target, save versus spells or `charmed` attaches indefinitely with the periodic re-save handler — interval by INT (3–8 monthly, 9–12 weekly, 13–18 daily; pinned: month = 30 days, week = 7 days; monsters have no INT score, so they default to the middle weekly band, override-correctable per monster). *Charm monster*: single creature of any HD, or 3d6 creatures of 3 HD or less, each saving; same charm effect and re-save machinery; undead immune.
    - **Cures and restoration.** *Cure light wounds*: heal 1d6+1 (capped at max, per `apply_healing`) or negate paralysis (releases `paralysed`-granting effects); *cure serious wounds*: 2d6+2, same shape. Their reversals inflict the same dice by touch attack. *Cure disease*: releases disease effects including mummy rot (unblocking healing) and its own reversal's disease; the green-slime kill mode is `manual` until the monster interaction matters. *Cause disease*: save versus spells or a disease effect attaches — death in 2d12 days (delayed-death handler), −2 attack modifier, natural healing at double time and magical healing blocked (the mummy-rot machinery, generalized). *Neutralize poison*: releases `poisoned` effects/condition; cast on a poison-dead creature within ten rounds of death revives them (pinned: the caller attests rounds-since-death until Phase 4's session owns the clock context). *Raise dead*: restores a dead human/demihuman if dead no longer than 4 days × (caster level − 7) — pinned to 0 days at level 7, RAW-faithful; revival sets 1 hp, removes `dead`, and attaches the two-week `weakened` effect (cannot attack or cast, half movement; released early by nothing — magical healing doesn't shorten it, per the page). *Remove fear*: releases `afraid`; against magical fear the subject saves versus spells with +1 per caster level to shake it — and *cause fear*: single target, save versus spells or `afraid` for 2 turns. *Stone to flesh*: releases `petrified` effects, closing Phase 2's "recoverable — stone is not dead" promise; *flesh to stone*: single target, save versus **paralysis** (the page names it) or `petrified` attaches permanently.
    - **Buffs and wards** (all through work item 6's modifiers). *Bless*: +1 attack, +1 damage, +1 morale to supplied allies (the "not yet in melee" condition is caller-asserted context, like braced/charging in Phase 2); *blight*: the −1s, each enemy saving versus spells to resist. *Striking*: +1d6 melee weapon damage and counts-as-magical for 1 turn — pinned to attach to the *wielder* (item instances carry no ids; the corner case of handing the enchanted weapon away mid-turn is accepted and registered). *Resist cold*/*resist fire*: element-scoped +2 saves and per-die damage reduction (min 1 per die), plus unharmed-by-nonmagical-element as prose note. *Shield*: the AC sets above. *Protection from evil* (both entries): +1 saves versus creatures of another alignment and −1 to their attacks against the warded; the enchanted/constructed/summoned melee ban ships as structured params consumed by Phase 4's battle machine (the kernel can't see who initiates melee), pinned and registered. *Protection from evil 10' radius* (both entries): the identical ward applied to the caster plus supplied allies (the 10' geometry is Phase 4's). *Protection from normal missiles*: the immunity gate above.
    - **Light and darkness.** The blind mode of *light*/*darkness*/*continual light*/*continual darkness* (all four entries): save versus spells or `blind` (Phase 2's cannot-attack hook) — for the duration on *light*, permanent-until-dispelled on *continual*. The illumination/darkness modes attach structured `attach_only` effects (radius, brightness, infravision-blocking as data) whose exploration meaning arrives in Phase 4. The cancel modes release the opposite effect (a targeted dispel: *light* ends *darkness*, mutually).
    - **Silence 15' radius**: cast on a creature — save versus spells; on a failure `silenced` attaches and the silence moves with them (casting validator consumes it); on a success the effect is stationary, which is Phase 4 space — pinned: the save-passed outcome attaches nothing in Phase 3 and reports the stationary form as manual, registered as a known gap until cells exist.
    - **Web**: supplied targets gain `entangled` with an escape countdown by STR — normal STR 2d4 turns, magically augmented STR above 18 4 rounds, giant strength 2 rounds (pinned: characters use their STR score; the augmented and giant tiers are caller/context assertions until such effects exist); the flammable-cube and blocking rules stay location prose for Phase 4.
    - **Dispel magic**: releases dispellable effects on the supplied targets (the 20' cube is Phase 4 geometry); per effect, if the recorded caster level exceeds the dispelling caster's, the effect survives on a 5%-per-level-difference roll (d100 from the `"magic"` stream); magic items are exempt by construction (Phase 5).
    - **Feeblemind**: a single arcane spell caster (validated as target having the `arcane_magic` tag — pinned; the page names magic-users and elves), save versus spells at −4 or `feebleminded` attaches permanently (blocks casting until dispelled/removed).
- Attach-only census (structured effects with pinned params, consumers later): *haste* (attacks ×2, movement ×2 — the battle machine and encumbrance consume), *invisibility* and *invisibility 10' radius* (`invisible`; the caster's own casts break it now, attack-breaking rides the battle machine), *mirror image* (1d4 images in effect state; a ledger helper pops one per incoming attack for the machine/game to call), *confusion* (3d6 targets, the 2d6 behavior table and per-round save versus spells above 2 HD as structured params for the action policy), *anti-magic shell* (marker condition params: blocks casting by and spell effects against the caster — validator honors the casting half now), *infravision*, *fly*, *levitate*, *water breathing* (movement/vision params for Phase 4). Everything else in the 106 — detections, utilities, walls, movement/teleportation, polymorphs, summon lifecycles, *quest*/*geas*, and kin — is `manual` prose, which is a supported cast, not a gap: the kernel consumes the slot and emits the event; the game or narrator resolves the fiction.

### 8. Turning undead — `core/spells.py` and `core/tables.py`

- `CombatTables` grows `turning: TurningTable`; `turning_result(cleric_level, hd_column) -> TurnResult` in `tables.py` (`fail` / threshold int / `turn` / `destroy`), rows clamped at `11+`, asserted verbatim against `Cleric.md`.
- Column mapping pinned: a monster's column is its `hit_dice.count` as a string, except count 2 with `asterisks > 0` maps to `2*` (the table's own footnote: "2 HD monsters with a special ability"), counts 7–9 share `7-9`, and counts above 9 have no column — turning **fails** against them (the printed table is the rule; the "referee may expand" footnote is game data territory). Modifiers don't shift columns (the mummy's 5+1 turns on column 5); the wight's `3*` is column 3 — the asterisk matters only at 2 HD.
- `turn_undead(cleric, definition, candidates, *, ledger, clock, allocator, registry, stream) -> TurnUndeadResult` — validated by the `turn_undead` class-ability tag. Procedure, pinned end to end: one 2d6 turn roll (the `"magic"` stream) is compared per candidate *type* against that type's cell — `—` types are unaffected, number types succeed when the roll meets the threshold, `T`/`D` succeed automatically. If any type succeeded, one 2d6 HD-pool roll follows; eligible monsters are affected lowest-HD-first (stable input order on ties), each costing its `hit_dice.count` (minimum 1, fixed bonuses dropped — the *sleep* convention); the pool stops at the first unaffordable monster (RAW: "Rolled Hit Dice that are not sufficient to affect a monster are wasted" — excess is wasted, not reallocated); at least one undead is always affected on a successful turn even when the pool rolls short (RAW minimum effect), pinned to the cheapest eligible monster. Affected monsters whose column says `D` die permanently (`kill(permanent=True)` — "instantly and permanently annihilated"); the rest gain the `turned` condition via an indefinite effect the encounter releases (flee behavior is the battle machine's, Phase 4).
- Only monsters bearing the `undead` category are candidates; the Phase 2 census (exactly ghoul, mummy, skeleton, spectre, the three vampires, wight, wraith, zombie) is the turning domain, and non-undead in the candidate list resolve as unaffected rather than rejecting (no-leak doctrine).
